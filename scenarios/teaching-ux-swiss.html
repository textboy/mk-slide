01 / DEFINITION

UX Is Not UI

User Experience is the total perception formed through every interaction with a product. UI is one layer; UX is the entire stack.

Usability

Can users complete tasks efficiently and without confusion?

Desirability

Does the product evoke positive emotional responses?

Accessibility

Is it inclusive for users of all abilities and contexts?

* Don Norman coined “User Experience” at Apple in 1993. Ref: “The Design of Everyday Things”, 2nd ed.

02 / HEURISTICS

Nielsen’s Usability Heuristics

10 general principles for interaction design (1994). Five key ones below.

01

Visibility of System Status

Loading spinners, progress bars, confirmation messages — tell users what’s happening.

02

Match Between System & Real World

Use familiar language. A “shopping cart” not a “purchase aggregation buffer.”

03

User Control & Freedom

Undo, redo, cancel. Every action should have a clear emergency exit.

04

Error Prevention

Disable “Submit” until form is valid. Confirmation dialogs for destructive actions.

05

Consistency & Standards

Follow platform conventions. iOS back swipe, Android back button — don’t fight muscle memory.

03 / WIREFRAMES

Wireframing & Visual Hierarchy

Why Wireframe?

  • ●Focus on structure, not aesthetics
  • ●Cheap to change early
  • ●Align stakeholders before pixels
  • ●Test information architecture

Hierarchy Tools

Size (bigger = more important), Color (contrast draws focus), Space (whitespace elevates), Position (top-left first).

CTA

Tools: Figma, Balsamiq, Whimsical, or pen & paper.

04 / TESTING

Usability Testing Protocol

01

Define Realistic Tasks

3–5 goal-oriented scenarios. Example: “Find a size M blue jacket and add it to your cart.”

02

Recruit 5 Participants

5 users find ~85% of usability problems (Nielsen & Landauer, 1993). Match target persona.

03

Think-Aloud & Record

Capture screen + audio. Never guide or hint. Ask “what are you thinking?” not “click here.”

04

Severity-Rate & Iterate

Critical → Major → Minor → Cosmetic. Fix critical first, re-test, repeat.

Remember: UX is iterative. Ship → test → learn → improve → repeat.
